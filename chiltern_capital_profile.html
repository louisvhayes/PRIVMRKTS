Chiltern Capital LLP was incorporated in October 2015 and is co-founded by Tom Phipps (formerly Livingstone Partners) and Alex Sheffield (formerly COO/CFO of a PE-backed business and CFO of a listed buy-and-build group). The firm takes a deliberately differentiated approach in a crowded lower mid-market: it invests its own funds rather than managing third-party LP capital, creating an unusually clear alignment of interest between the Chiltern team and the management teams it backs.

The firm targets profitable UK and Ireland businesses with £2m–£10m+ EBIT, operating across all B2B sectors. It will take minority or majority positions and has no set time horizon to exit — an important differentiator from traditional PE funds constrained by 10-year fund lifecycles. Chiltern invests between £10 million and £40 million of equity per deal, with flexibility to invest additional capital to support growth plans.

Chiltern's core philosophy
"We invest our own funds which sets us apart from typical private equity investors. As it's our capital, there is a clear sense of alignment and commitment between us and the management teams we back. Every member of our firm personally invests in each deal — there is a clear sense of being in it together from deal completion through to the eventual exit."

Investment Approach

Chiltern describes itself as having "the dynamism of an independent investor with the laser focus of a trade player." The firm is naturally curious and takes deep market knowledge seriously — seeking to understand sectors before investing, then remaining actively involved without interfering in day-to-day operations.

Active but non-interfering
Chiltern takes board seats and plays an active governance role, but does not interfere in day-to-day operations. The firm's style is to agree how it can best add value — focusing on M&A origination and execution, strategic guidance, and senior-level team augmentation — then deliver those specific capabilities without overstepping into operational management.
Partnership mentality
Every Chiltern team member personally invests in each deal. The firm's approach is explicitly entrepreneurial — it "thinks like entrepreneurs, not asset managers." Communication is described as clear and direct: "we tell it how it is, good or bad." This straight-talking style is intentionally designed to build trust with founders and owner-managers.
M&A-led value creation
Buy-and-build is a primary value creation lever. Chiltern's directors and portfolio team work closely with management to identify, structure and execute bolt-on acquisitions. Alistair Gray leads inorganic growth strategies across the portfolio. The firm brings deal structuring expertise, financial capacity, and an adviser network to accelerate acquisition programmes.
Permanent capital advantage
Unlike fund-managed PE, Chiltern is not constrained by an LP-driven exit timeline. There is no artificial 10-year clock forcing portfolio sales at inopportune times. This is an explicit competitive advantage when pitching to founders who want certainty about the quality of long-term ownership rather than a revolving door of PE buyers every 5 years.
Hands-on portfolio support
Jon Rawlings and Michael Coupland joined as Portfolio Directors in 2024, bringing dedicated resource specifically focused on value creation, operational improvement, and strategic delivery within portfolio companies. They serve directly on boards and work closely with management teams on growth plan execution — not just monitoring.
Flexible deal structuring
Chiltern considers both minority and majority positions and adapts deal structure to fit the situation. It can deploy follow-on capital without fundraising constraints. This flexibility — combined with the ability to move quickly with no LP approval processes — gives the firm a structural advantage in competitive lower mid-market processes.

Chiltern engages in three primary types of investment situations:

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦
Founder / family exit
Working with a founder or family shareholders seeking a smooth exit who want to transition ownership to a team they trust — either in full or partially while retaining a stake. Succession planning is a key theme in Chiltern's origination activity.
⚖️
Shareholder misalignment
Where shareholders have different timescales or ambitions and ultimately want different outcomes. Chiltern provides capital and structure to resolve the misalignment without disrupting the business — a common situation in family businesses and partnerships.
🏢
Corporate carve-out
Corporates or groups looking to sell a non-core business unit. Chiltern backs the incumbent management team to run the business independently, providing capital and strategic support. The Strata Products carve-out from Berry Global Inc. (2024) is a flagship example.
🚀
Management buy-out (MBO)
Backing management teams who wish to buy their own business — providing the capital structure for the MBO and then supporting the team to accelerate growth organically or via acquisition. Chiltern actively partners with management on the buy-out thesis from day one.
📈
Growth capital
Providing minority or majority growth capital to support teams developing their business — organically, through roll-out, or via targeted bolt-on acquisitions. Chiltern specifically aims to support companies with significant growth and development potential that are already established and profitable.
🔄
Replacement / de-risking
Entrepreneurs who want to de-risk and crystallise value but still have the ambition to achieve more. Chiltern can buy out a portion of the founder's stake while leaving them with meaningful equity in the next phase of growth — aligning interests for the continued journey.

What Sets Chiltern Apart

In a lower mid-market PE landscape crowded with fund-managed investors, Chiltern's structural and cultural differentiation is meaningful. The firm's pitch to founders is built on three genuinely distinctive attributes that most lower mid-market PE firms cannot credibly replicate.

✓ Permanent own-capital model
  • Invests from its own balance sheet — not managing third-party LP capital with fund lifecycle constraints
  • No artificial 10-year fund exit deadline forcing portfolio company sales at the wrong time
  • Every Chiltern team member personally co-invests in every deal — genuine skin in the game at all levels
  • Full alignment with management: Chiltern only makes money when the business succeeds, not from management fees
  • Can deploy follow-on capital without a fundraising process or LP approval — faster, simpler execution
✓ Founder-centric partnership approach
  • Non-interfering: actively supports strategy, M&A and governance but does not run portfolio companies
  • Direct, honest communication — described explicitly as "we tell it how it is, good or bad"
  • Dedicated Portfolio Directors (Jon Rawlings, Michael Coupland) who sit on boards and execute growth plans
  • Ryan Robson (Chairman) brings founder and large-scale buy-and-build experience beyond financial PE background
  • Flexible on minority or majority — can structure around founder's specific goals, not a one-size-fits-all buyout
✓ Deep B2B sector expertise
  • Consistent focus on business services, industrials and technology services — not a generalist fund with scattered bets
  • Team backgrounds across Transaction Services (PwC, Deloitte, EY, KPMG, Grant Thornton) and operational roles
  • Alex Sheffield's COO/CFO operating background gives Chiltern credibility in conversations with management teams
  • 10-year track record of consistent activity in the lower mid-market provides deep relationship networks with advisers
  • Buy-and-build execution capability — dedicated inorganic growth support from Alistair Gray and the portfolio team
⚠ Limitations to acknowledge
  • Smaller team (22 people) limits simultaneous deal capacity vs larger PE houses with deeper origination resource
  • Own-capital model means no "dry powder" announcements or headline fund sizes — may appear smaller to market
  • Purely UK & Ireland focused — no international PE network for cross-border deals or international buy-and-build
  • Lower-mid market focus (£2–10m EBIT) limits addressable deal universe and may constrain scale of platform exits
  • Minority stakes in some situations may limit governance influence compared to majority-only investors
Assessment
"Chiltern Capital occupies a well-defined and defensible niche in the UK lower mid-market. Its permanent capital model and whole-team co-investment structure create a genuinely differentiated pitch to founders — particularly family business owners and management buy-out teams who have been burned by the 10-year fund exit clock of traditional PE. A decade of consistent activity, 53+ investments and a growing team with dedicated portfolio support resource suggest a firm that has moved well beyond the start-up phase and into an established, repeatable model."
