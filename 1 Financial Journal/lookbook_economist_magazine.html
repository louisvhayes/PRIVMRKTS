Café

Café Noēma

📍 Soho, London

Café Noēma is the kind of place that gets posted on Pinterest before anyone has even finished their coffee. Marble surfaces catch the eastern morning light, the menu is small and perfectly formed, and the matcha latte arrives looking like a still-life painting. It is entirely possible to sit here for three hours and feel completely justified about it.

📸 Shot East window, Table 4. Between 9–11am the light is extraordinary. Shoot overhead for the latte art, eye-level for the pastry plate. The white-tiled wall behind the bar is a clean backdrop for portraits.
💷 Price £10–16 per person. Matcha latte £5.50, avocado toast £9, croissant £3.80. Student discount: 10% Mon–Thu with valid ID.
🕐 Hours Mon–Fri 7:30am–5pm · Sat–Sun 9am–6pm

The Archive — amber hour
Bar

The Archive Bar

📍 Shoreditch, London

Low ceilings, exposed brick, amber pendant lights, and a natural wine list written on a chalkboard that changes weekly. The Archive feels like a secret even when it is full. The kind of bar you discover on a Tuesday and spend the rest of the week telling people about. Go early — the seats at the back bar are the ones you want.

📸 Shot Back bar corner stool. The amber bulb overhead creates a film-grain glow. Use portrait mode, turn off flash. The chalkboard menu makes a great background for a sit-down portrait shot.
💷 Price £22–35 per person. Natural wine glass from £7, sharing plates £6–12. No student discount but happy hours 5–7pm daily cuts 20% off drinks.
🕐 Hours Tue–Sun 5pm–1am · Closed Mondays

Corner table — afternoon light
Restaurant

Brasserie Lumière

📍 Saint-Germain, Paris

Floor-to-ceiling windows, cream linen tablecloths, and a menu so classically French it borders on a love letter to butter. Brasserie Lumière earns its name — on a clear afternoon the whole room glows. The prix-fixe student lunch (€28 for three courses) is one of the best value meals in the 6th arrondissement. Reserve at least a week ahead.

📸 Shot Corner table, window side. Arrive before 12:30pm for the best light through the tall windows. The entrée plate — usually a fish tartare — is among the most photographable dishes in Paris. Shoot with the window behind you, not in front.
💷 Price €55–75 à la carte / €28 student prix-fixe. Wine from €9 a glass. Lunch only for the student menu — show ISIC card on arrival.
🕐 Hours Daily 12pm–3pm & 7pm–11pm
View on Google Maps ↗
"If Paris had a screensaver, it would be the light through Lumière's windows at noon."
— The Look Book, Spring 2026
Studio Kitchen — the long table
Café

Studio Kitchen

📍 Dalston, London

An all-white converted railway arch with terracotta dishes, hanging dried herbs, and a menu that changes with the season. Studio Kitchen is built for the long brunch — there is nowhere to be in a hurry here. The shakshuka is the reason people move to Dalston. Bring your camera and your most patient friend.

📸 Shot Long communal table, centre. The all-white walls bounce light evenly — no bad angles. The shakshuka in the black cast-iron pan against the white surface is a guaranteed keeper. Saturday morning 10–11am is peak light.
💷 Price £14–22 per person. Shakshuka £12, granola bowl £9, coffee from £3.50. No bookings — walk-in only. Queue forms by 9am on weekends.
🕐 Hours Sat–Sun 9am–4pm · Mon–Fri 8am–3pm

Atelier Sept — concrete and candlelight
Restaurant

Atelier Sept

📍 Pigalle, Paris

Industrial architecture softened by candlelight. Atelier Sept serves a tasting menu of seven courses built entirely around what arrived from the market that morning. The room — exposed concrete, blackened steel, and raw oak — photographs like a fashion editorial without trying. Booking two weeks ahead is not an exaggeration.

📸 Shot Table by the north wall. The exposed concrete behind lit only by the single candle on each table creates a dramatic, editorial portrait background. Shoot the amuse-bouche courses — they arrive on raw slate boards. Long exposure if you have a tripod, or bump ISO to 1600.
💷 Price €90–110 per person for tasting menu. Wine pairing €45 extra. No student discount but the lunch menu (Fri only) is €35 for four courses.
🕐 Hours Tue–Sat 7:30pm–11pm · Fri lunch 12:30–2:30pm
View on Google Maps ↗
"A tasting menu that reads like a poem and photographs like one too."

La Serre — the greenhouse ceiling
Bar · Spot

La Serre

📍 Fitzrovia, London

A Victorian greenhouse reimagined as a late-night wine bar. Ivy climbs the ceiling, candles melt slowly on wooden tables, and the aperol spritz arrives in a glass the size of a small fishbowl. La Serre is what you show people when they say London has no atmosphere. It has all of it, in one room.

📸 Shot Shoot upward through the ivy ceiling. The glass roof panels at dusk create a natural gradient from blue to amber. The long wooden bar at golden hour (6–7pm) is exceptional. Bring a wide-angle lens if you have one — the space rewards it.
💷 Price £25–40 per person. Aperol spritz £11, natural wine from £8, small plates £7–14. 15% student discount Sunday–Thursday.
🕐 Hours Mon–Thu 5pm–12am · Fri–Sat 4pm–1am · Sun 3pm–11pm

Kombu — the six-table dining room
Restaurant

Kombu

📍 Notting Hill, London

Six tables. A menu of Japanese-inspired small plates that changes entirely twice a week. No reservations, no website, and a queue that forms reliably from 6pm despite none of this being advertised anywhere. Kombu is the kind of restaurant that builds a cult following in three months. Deservedly so — the scallop with yuzu butter alone justifies everything.

📸 Shot Counter seats at the open kitchen. Watch the chef plate — those are the five seconds you want to shoot. The warm kitchen light on white ceramic is the ideal condition. Ask nicely and they will hold a plate for a moment.
💷 Price £35–50 per person. Small plates £7–16, sake from £6 a glass. No discounts but portions are generous and the BYO policy (£3 corkage) makes it remarkably affordable.
🕐 Hours Wed–Sun 6pm–10:30pm · Walk-in only

"Six tables. One queue. Zero regrets."

Pavilion Social — the lakeside terrace
Café · Spot

Pavilion Social

📍 Victoria Park, London

On the edge of Victoria Park Lake, Pavilion Social has somehow become the most reliably beautiful café in East London. The architecture is modest — white painted wood, outdoor benches, a simple chalkboard menu — but the lakeside setting means that on a clear morning the light is genuinely extraordinary. The croissant is the best in E3. Full stop.

📸 Shot Lakeside bench, early morning. Arrive before 9am on a clear day — the mist on the lake behind a coffee cup is a perfect image. The café exterior itself, white against the green park, photographs beautifully at any time. Reflections in the lake at golden hour are a bonus.
💷 Price £8–14 per person. Filter coffee £3.20, cortado £3.80, croissant £3.50, avocado toast £9. Cash preferred, card accepted.
🕐 Hours Daily 8am–5pm (weather permitting)
