How to read this document

This document is built from Mobeus’s public website, investment pages, deal announcements and the Summer 2025 “Inside Out” PDF. It is therefore strongest on investments that Mobeus still highlights publicly, and on exits where it published return data. It is weaker on any historical investments that are no longer featured clearly on the public site.

Important caveat: this should be read as a public-materials reconstruction of Mobeus 1 and Mobeus 2, not a definitive LP reporting pack. For several current investments, Mobeus discloses the value-creation plan and some trading or M&A milestones, but does not publicly disclose unrealised fund returns. Where that is the case, the document explicitly says so.
In other words: for realised deals, you often get MoC and IRR; for unrealised deals, you usually get strategy, operational actions and selected KPIs — but not current mark-to-market returns.

Mobeus 1 — what it is

Mobeus 1 is the firm’s first institutional small-cap buyout fund. Mobeus says it closed in 2017. By Summer 2025, the firm described Mobeus 1 as the fund that was “being realised”, with 7 investments realised, 7 still in portfolio, a top quartile vintage, 5x realised MoC and 2x already returned in cash. That makes it the mature harvesting vehicle in the current Mobeus story.

The public material does not give a hard public “end date” for Mobeus 1. However, because it closed in 2017 and because traditional private equity buyout funds are commonly structured with lives of roughly 10 to 12 years, the natural interpretation is that Mobeus 1 is now in its realisation/distribution phase, with exits expected over the next few years and final wind-down likely only after remaining assets are sold.

Mobeus 2 — what it is

Mobeus 2 LP is the successor institutional buyout fund. Mobeus announced a £115m first close against a £175m target in November 2021 and said the fund would invest £5m to £20m in a mix of control and minority stakes in UK small-cap MBOs. By Summer 2025, Mobeus described Mobeus 2 as the fund “being invested”, with 8 investments completed, average deal size above £12.5m, one £20m investment, and deployment completed within four years.

Again, there is no public website disclosure of the precise legal termination date. On a normal private equity timetable, a 2021 launch would often imply a natural end somewhere around 2031–2033 depending on extensions. But that is an inference from the generic PE fund model, not a Mobeus-specific public commitment. Summer 2025 materials also indicated the fund still expected to deploy more than £30m over the following six months, and Mobeus’s January 2026 G=mc2 page still labels that investment as Fund: Mobeus 2.

04

What the realised deals say about Mobeus’s playbook

The realised Mobeus 1 exits show a pattern. Mobeus tends to make money not from one single trick, but from combining management backing, operational professionalisation, targeted M&A, international growth and board-level resourcing. The very biggest outcome — Star Brands — is a branded-goods version of that formula. The consulting and services exits — Access, Kinneir Dufort, The Boundary — are specialist-services versions of the same formula.

Most spectacular winner
Star Brands: sales up 5x, EBITDA up 15x, sold for £345m plus earnout. This is the clearest illustration of Mobeus’s “identify a latent growth lever and back management hard” model.
Best published IRR
Access Partnership: 4.4x MoC and 64% IRR, showing that a shorter-hold specialist consultancy success can generate very high annualised returns.
Consistent pattern
Advantedge, Ludlow, Kinneir Dufort, The Boundary all show public evidence of operational growth plus strong exit metrics, rather than passive holding.
The public return data suggests Mobeus 1 has been driven by a handful of strong winners, supported by several solid 2–4x outcomes — exactly the kind of distribution many successful lower mid-market buyout funds rely on.
05

When are Mobeus 1 and Mobeus 2 supposed to end or pay out investors?

The key honest answer is: Mobeus’s public website does not state the exact legal termination dates or waterfall schedule for either fund. Those details normally sit in private fund documents rather than public marketing pages.

What we can say from public evidence is this:

Mobeus 1
Closed in 2017 and is explicitly described in Summer 2025 as the fund that is being realised. Mobeus says it had already returned 2x in cash, had 7 realisations completed, and expected the remaining realisation programme to run over the next few years.
Mobeus 2
Launched in 2021 and was still actively investing in 2025–2026. Summer 2025 materials say the fund was being invested; January 2026 public pages still list new deals such as G=mc2 as Fund: Mobeus 2.

From the generic private equity model, a buyout fund often has a life of roughly 10 years, commonly with extension rights. So the sensible public-market interpretation is that Mobeus 1 is in late harvesting mode, while Mobeus 2 is still in the investment and early value-creation phase and would usually distribute meaningfully later in the cycle rather than immediately.

06

Bottom line

Mobeus 1 is now best understood as the realised-and-realising proof of concept: public materials show strong exits, high realised multiples and a portfolio still being harvested. Mobeus 2 is the active deployment vehicle: a growing set of UK lower mid-market investments where the public evidence focuses on boards, strategy, hiring, M&A and operational scaling — but not yet on disclosed returns.
Practical implication: if you want the cleanest return evidence, look to Mobeus 1 exits. If you want to understand how Mobeus operates today, look to Mobeus 2 current holdings and the operational plans attached to them.
